How Real Annual ROI is calculated

Incremental IRR vs. Age-62 baseline. Delaying = investment: cost = foregone benefits during wait; return = lifetime surplus of higher household SS. We find annualized real rate r where NPV of incremental cash flows = 0. Positive = waiting beats age-62 in real terms.

Section 3 — Taxable Social Security Amount

IRS Publication 915 — two-tier formula

Provisional income (PI) = all non-SS income + 50% of household SS

Married Filing Jointly:
Exempt PI < $32,000 → $0 taxable SS
Tier 1 $32k–$44k → min(50%×(PI−$32k), 50%×SS)
Tier 2 PI > $44k → min($6,000+85%×(PI−$44k), 85%×SS)

Single (after John passes):
Exempt PI < $25,000 → $0
Tier 1 $25k–$34k → min(50%×(PI−$25k), 50%×SS)
Tier 2 PI > $34k → min($4,500+85%×(PI−$34k), 85%×SS)
⚠️ Frozen thresholds: Set in the 1980s–90s, never COLA-adjusted. As income rises with inflation, more SS becomes taxable each year.
⚡ SS Torpedo: Every extra dollar of non-SS income can push up to $1.85 into taxation — the dollar itself plus up to $0.85 more SS.
